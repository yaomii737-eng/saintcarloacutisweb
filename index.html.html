Blessed Carlo Acutis

×

This portrait shows Carlo Acutis as a teenager, shortly before his death at age 15. Despite his young age, Carlo had already developed a deep spiritual maturity and unwavering faith that would inspire millions.

Did you know?

Carlo was known for his joyful personality and infectious smile. He often said "I'm happy to die because I've lived my life without wasting even a minute on things that don't please God."

Carlo's Early Years

×

From a very young age, Carlo showed an extraordinary attraction to Jesus in the Eucharist. He would often ask to visit churches and was fascinated by the tabernacle.

Early Signs of Holiness

  • ‚Ä¢ Asked for First Communion at age 7 (younger than usual)
  • ‚Ä¢ Prayed the rosary daily from childhood
  • ‚Ä¢ Showed compassion for the poor and homeless
  • ‚Ä¢ Had a natural love for prayer and silence

The Acutis Family

×

Carlo's parents, Andrea Acutis and Antonia Salzano, were initially non-practicing Catholics. However, Carlo's deep faith and example led them back to active participation in the Church.

Family Transformation

Carlo's mother, Antonia, often speaks about how her son's faith transformed their entire family. She said, "Carlo taught us to pray and to love Jesus in the Eucharist."

First Holy Communion

×

Carlo received his First Holy Communion at age 7, which was younger than the typical age. From that day forward, he attended daily Mass whenever possible and developed an intense devotion to the Eucharist.

Carlo's Words

"The Eucharist is my highway to heaven. When we face the sun, we get a tan... but when we stand before Jesus in the Eucharist, we become saints."

Eucharistic Miracles Website

×

At age 11, Carlo began creating a website documenting Eucharistic miracles from around the world. He taught himself programming and web design to share his passion for the Eucharist with others.

Technical Achievement

Carlo's website featured over 130 documented Eucharistic miracles, complete with historical research, photographs, and scientific evidence. It became a valuable resource for Catholics worldwide.

His Mission

"I want to use the internet to evangelize and bring people closer to Jesus, especially through the Eucharist."

Beatification Ceremony

×

On October 10, 2020, Carlo Acutis was beatified in Assisi, Italy, by Cardinal Agostino Vallini, representing Pope Francis. He became the first millennial to be declared blessed by the Catholic Church.

Historic Moment

Thousands of young people from around the world attended the ceremony, seeing Carlo as a model for living faith in the digital age.

Patron Saint

Carlo has been proposed as the patron saint of the internet and computer programmers, reflecting his innovative use of technology for evangelization.

Carlo's Resting Place

×

Carlo's body rests in the Sanctuary of the Renunciation in Assisi, Italy. His tomb has become a pilgrimage destination for young people and families from around the world.

Pilgrimage Site

Thousands of pilgrims visit Carlo's tomb each year, many reporting spiritual experiences and renewed faith. The site has become especially popular with young people.

Incorrupt Body

Carlo's body was found to be incorrupt when exhumed for the beatification process, which the Church considers a sign of holiness.
